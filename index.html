About the Book

"Mein Kampf" is a book written by Adolf Hitler while he was imprisoned in 1924. The title translates to "My Struggle." It combines autobiography and political ideology.

Historical Background

Hitler wrote the book after the failed Beer Hall Putsch in Germany. In the book, he outlines his political views, including extreme nationalism and racist ideas that later became central to Nazi ideology.

Impact and Legacy

The ideas in the book played a role in shaping the policies of Nazi Germany. Today, the book is studied mainly in historical and academic contexts to understand how extremist propaganda contributed to one of the darkest periods in modern history.
